Welcome to Morocco!

Introduction

-Morocco is a country located in the North-West of Africa, between two maritime facades (the Atlantic Ocean and the Mediterranean).
-Its capital is Rabat, and the metropolitan cities are Casablanca, Marrakech and Agadir.
-The official language in Morocco is Arabic, but French and English are widely spoken.
-The population of Morocco is approximately 38 million and the area is 710,850 km2.
-The currency in Morocco is the Moroccan Dirham and the official animal of Morocco is the Atlas lion.

Gastronomy

-There are several dishes to eat in Morocco, since Moroccan cuisine is very rich and varied.
-The most popular dishes are the seven vegetable couscous, meat tagine, seafood pastilla, harira soup, rfissa, batbout and stuffed Chicken Briwat.
-Mint tea holds an important place in life and social relations in Morocco because it is the drink of friendship and hospitality.
-Examples of petit fours in Morocco are Chebakia with sesame seeds and honey, Fekkas with almonds, and Ghriyba with icing sugar.

Traditional clothing

-Traditional clothing in Morocco includes djellabas, caftans, gandoura and babouches.
-Men wear the ja-bad-or, the kamis, the djellabas and the masculine babouches.
-These Moroccan clothes represent the cultural and artistic diversity of the country.
-We mostly wear them during special celebration times like weddings, Eid, and Ramadan.

Architecture

-Morocco is a country beautifully divided in art and architecture.
-In houses we find tiles with patterns of a variety of invigorating colors, traditional fountains in the heart of the living room, wrought iron stair railings, hand-woven Andalus rugs, suspended mosaic lamps, etc.
-Moroccan craftsmanship is divided into four main traditional sectors which are wood, metals, textiles and leather.
-With these resources, Moroccan craftsmen make pottery, weaving, brassware with copper, ironwork, leather goods, jewelry and embroidery with textiles.
